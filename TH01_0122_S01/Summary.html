Outlier Analysis Methodology:
Gene expression outlier analysis uses a statistical test to identify genes with unusual expression levels in the patient's tumor2. We employ two types of outlier analysis: the pan-cancer analysis is performed by comparing a patient's tumor RNA sequencing profile to the RNA sequencing profiles of all tumors in the reference compendium (Appendix A3).
The consensus outlier analysis versus personalized cohorts is performed by comparing the patient's tumor RNA sequencing profile to the RNA sequencing profiles of four different sets of similar tumors and selecting only outliers that appear in at least two of the four sets. These sets are defined as follows: "Focus Sample Diagnosed Disease" - all tumor samples listed as the same disease as the focus sample; "First Degree Most Correlated Samples" - all samples that have a Spearman correlation with the focus sample that meets a threshold; "First and Second Degree Most Correlated Samples" - first degree most correlated samples and in addition each of those samples' first degree most correlated; and "Diseases of Top Six Most Correlated Samples above Threshold" - all tumor samples listed as the same disease of one or more of the 6 most highly-correlated reference samples, provided they pass the threshold.
Genes identified by the outlier analysis are further examined for statistical enrichment of functional categories and signaling pathways using the Molecular Signatures Database3. Significantly enriched pathways containing cancer drug targets, as defined by the Drug Gene Interaction Database4 are reported below under "Druggable Pathways" and the full list of pan-cancer and pan-disease gene expression outliers is provided in Appendix A4. Genes whose protein products could be targeted by available therapies are listed under "Druggable Genes".

Appendices:

A.1 Tumor Map Global Placement

Tumor Map provides a visualization of the genomic similarity of tumor samples. In the Tumor Map, each "dot" represents a sample, and samples are grouped based on RNA sequencing derived gene expression levels. Tumor Map is a research tool and is intended as an adjunct with other molecular diagnostic information to gain insight into how a given individual's tumor RNA sequencing profile compares to those of tumors in the reference cohort. Tumor Map should not be used as a stand-alone diagnostic tool.

A.2 Reference Compendium and Protocol

The compendium used was the Treehouse Reference Compendium Version 5. This reference compendium was constructed in February 2018 and includes 9806 tumors from The Cancer Genome Atlas (TCGA) project, 784 tumors from the Therapeutically Applicable Research to Generate Effective Treatments (TARGET) project, 586 tumors from other repository data sets, and 164 tumors from the Treehouse data set, for a total of 11340 tumor samples.
Treehouse Tertiary Analysis Protocol revision 815d19b4bb9ad9ee455e3ed4ec9b188cbe32490d was used (https://github.com/UCSC-Treehouse/protocol/tree/V8.0.1).

A.5 Quality Control Note

The RNA-Seq data were deemed to be of sufficient quality to perform Tumor Map and Gene Expression Outlier analyses. The quality was assessed by enumerating the number of Uniquely Mapped Exonic Non-Duplicate (UMEND) reads. UC Santa Cruz Treehouse group determined that it is necessary to have 10M UMEND reads for accurate comparative RNA-Seq analysis, and the sample meets this requirement.

A.6 Library Construction Note REMOVE THIS NOTE FOR polyA SAMPLES

The RNA-Seq library for this case was constructed using a ribo-deplete protocol. Most RNA-Seq datasets in the Treehouse compendium are constructed using a polyA protocol, and this library construction method is assumed in our computational analysis. The pathway analysis findings for this case were manually curated to exclude non-poly-adenylated transcripts.
